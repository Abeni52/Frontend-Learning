Doro Wet

Doro Wot is a spicy chicken stew garnished with hard-boiled eggs that have been steeped in the sauce. It's often called the national dish of Ethiopia, featured both at family celebrations and on almost every restaurant menu.

Ingredients

  • Berbere
  • Onions
  • Niter Kibbeh
  • Chicken
  • Hard-Boiled Eggs

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Onions:

    2. Heat the niter kibbeh (or ghee) in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.

    Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are caramelized, about 30 minutes.

  2. Add the Aromatics:

    3. Stir in the garlic, ginger, and berbere and cook for 2 minutes.

  3. Incorporate the Berbere:

    4. Add the berbere spice blend, ground cardamom, and nutmeg. Stir well to coat the onions and cook for a few minutes, allowing the spices to bloom.

    Add the chicken pieces and stir to coat them in the sauce. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Deglaze and Simmer:

    5. Pour in the chicken broth and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

    Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Cook for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Finish and Serve:

    6. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

    Serve the chicken berbere with hard-boiled eggs and enjoy!
